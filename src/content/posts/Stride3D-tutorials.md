I have been exploring game development with the Stride3D game engine and started recording some tutorials along the way.

The goal of this series is not to present a massive polished course. It is more of a practical learning log: small steps, focused examples, and notes from building things directly inside the engine.

You can learn more about the engine on the [official Stride3D homepage](https://www.stride3d.net/).

## Why Stride3D

- It uses C#, which fits naturally with my existing .NET background.
- It gives me a way to explore game development without leaving the ecosystem I already enjoy.
- It is interesting for learning engine concepts, rendering, scenes, entities, components, and gameplay structure.
- It feels like a good space to experiment in public and document what I learn.

Stride3D is especially appealing to me because it sits close to the type of programming I already like: structured, code-driven, and flexible enough to let me understand what is happening under the hood.

## What can you expect from the tutorials

- beginner-friendly walkthroughs
- small experiments instead of huge theory dumps
- C#-oriented explanations
- practical examples inside the editor
- notes about what worked, what confused me, and what I would improve next

The tutorials are aimed at people who are curious about game development, especially if they already know a bit of C# or want to learn through practical examples.
